PHYSICAL WELLNESS

 

Physical self care pertains to taking care of our outer bodies. A healthy body can do so much for us; there are biological processes going on inside of us, keeping us alive, fighting illnesses, digesting food, and so much more – every single second! And we don’t even have to think about it! We’ve been blessed with this superpower, so it’s our job to take whatever steps we can to keep our body functioning properly. By keeping our bodies healthy, we have the potential to enjoy our lives and do amazing things!

 



❤️ WATER ❤️

Our body is 60% water, and every day, we lose fluid in urine, sweat, and even breathing! With water being one of the most vital aspects of our life, we need to make sure we are drinking enough of it everyday.


TIP #1

Drink 11.5 cups of water everyday (15.5 cups for men). You can keep track with our water tracker here!

TIP #2

Always keep a water bottle with you! Take sips throughout the day, even if you’re not thirsty!

ACTIVITY

Go drink a glass of water. Then, fill a water bottle to carry along with you for the rest of the day!







❤️ DIET ❤️

Eating a healthy diet is all about feeling great, having more energy, improving your health, and boosting your mood. It protects you against many chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes and cancer. Eating a variety of foods and consuming less salt, sugars and saturated and industrially-produced trans-fats are essential for a healthy diet. Good food = good mood!


TIP #1

Choose healthy snacks! These keep you satiated, thus preventing unhealthy cravings.

TIP #2

Homemade over eating out! Trust us, you’re much more likely to consume unhealthy food if you’re eating out.

ACTIVITY

Make yourself a healthy dinner tonight!







❤️ EXERCISE ❤️

Exercise has endless benefits! It strengthens your heart and improves circulation, reducing the risks of heart diseases; it boosts energy, blocks negative thoughts and distracts you from daily worries; and the more obvious one — it helps you burn calories! Moreover, research shows that physical activity boosts self-esteem, mood, and sleep quality — basically everything we’re trying to achieve here. Exercise is key!


TIP #1

Incorporate outdoor walks/jogs into your routine. They’re much more effective than you think!

TIP #2

Establish an exercise routine, and try your best to stick to it as much as possible! 3-5 times a week is usually recommended.

ACTIVITY #1

Practice your favorite sport!




ACTIVITY #2

Go outdoors for a nice walk/run!







❤️ SLEEP ❤️

To put it simply, sleep is vital. Sleep is necessary for your mind and body to recharge, and the consequences of continued sleep deprivation go far beyond mere fatigue and irritability. Some of the most serious potential problems associated with chronic sleep deprivation are high blood pressure, diabetes, heart attack, heart failure or stroke. Moreover, sleep is a luxury often taken for granted by many. 50 – 70 million people in the USA alone suffer from insomnia. If you are able to sleep well, be thankful that you can.


TIP #1

Set fixed bedtimes and wake up times you can stick to, making sure you get at least 7-8 hours of sleep each night!

TIP #2

Utilize our amazing sleep log to keep track of your sleep schedule!

TIP #3

Log off your phone at least 15-30 mins before bed; screen time right before bed has been proven to disrupt sleep quality.




ACTIVITY

Take a nap!







❤️ SKINCARE ❤️

Taking good care of your skin is important for more than just your appearance. As the largest organ you have, your skin is essential to your general health. Although the effects of a good (or not) skincare routine may be negligible when you are younger, the effects are much more visible and irreversible once you get older. This is why it is so important to have a well-thought-out skin care routine. Skincare is like an investment in yourself —you’ll reap the benefits as you age!


TIP #1

Find products that work for you. Remember, less is more!

TIP #2

Use our amazing habit tracker to make sure you do your skincare everyday!

ACTIVITY

If you do not already have a skincare routine, set an appointment with your local dermatologist to help you devise one that suits your needs!
